Frequently Asked Questions

Here are answers to some of the most common questions I receive as a MERN stack full-stack developer. If you don’t see your question here, feel free to reach out—I’m happy to help!

1.What services do you offer? ^
I specialize in full-stack web development using the MERN stack (MongoDB, Express.js, React, Node.js). I can build responsive web applications, RESTful APIs, e-commerce platforms, dashboards, and custom solutions. From front-end design to back-end logic and database integration, I handle the complete development cycle.
2.How does the design process work? ^
The process begins with understanding your project requirements and goals. Then, I design the architecture, set up the database, and build the front-end and back-end. I provide regular updates, perform testing, and deploy the application once it’s ready. Collaboration and feedback are key throughout the process.
3.How long does a project usually take? ^
The timeline depends on the project’s complexity. Smaller projects, like a personal portfolio or a landing page, may take 1-2 weeks. Medium projects, like a CRUD application or dashboard, can take 3-4 weeks. Larger projects, like an e-commerce platform or a SaaS app, may take 6-8 weeks. A detailed timeline will be shared after discussing your requirements.
4.What do I need to provide before starting a project? ^
I’ll need information about your business, project goals, desired features, and any design preferences or branding assets (like logos or color schemes). For content-driven apps, text, images, or data files will also be required. Don’t worry—I’ll guide you through everything step by step.
5.Do you offer revisions? ^
Yes! I make sure the final product meets your expectations. During development, I incorporate feedback, and after deployment, I can provide updates or feature additions. Support for bug fixes and improvements is also available after project delivery.
6.How do I get started? ^
Reach out through my contact form or email me directly. We’ll discuss your project, timeline, and requirements. After that, I’ll provide a proposal and development plan so we can get started.
